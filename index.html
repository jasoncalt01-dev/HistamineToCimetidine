What is Histamine?

Histamine is a chemical messenger released by the immune system to serve several important functions, best known for triggering allergic and anaphylactic reactions.

Allergies occur when your body reacts to a foreign protein, known as an allergen. Although these proteins are typically harmless, an allergic individual's immune system mistakenly identifies them as threats and overreacts [1].

This overreaction triggers a chain of events that lead to the release of histamine, resulting in allergy symptoms. These symptoms are often localized to a specific part of the body, but in severe cases, the reaction can escalate to anaphylaxisA severe allergic reaction that might be fatal. Symptoms include swelling, wheezing, shortness of breath and difficulty swallowing. [2], which affects multiple body systems.
